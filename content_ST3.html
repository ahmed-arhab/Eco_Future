Climate Change Impact on Ecosystems

Authored by Student 3

In this article:

  • - Biodiversity Loss

Biodiversity Loss & Habitat Destruction

Rising global temperatures are fundamentally altering ecosystems around the world, forcing species out of their natural habitats. Many plants and animals are migrating towards higher altitudes or the poles in search of cooler climates. Unfortunately, species that cannot adapt or relocate quickly enough face the severe threat of extinction.

Ocean Acidification and Coral Bleaching

The world's oceans absorb about 30% of the carbon dioxide that is released in the atmosphere. The excess CO2 alters the chemistry of the ocean, making it more acidic. This acidification dissolves the calcium carbonate shells of marine organisms, severely threatening coral reefs—often called the rainforests of the sea—which support 25% of all marine biodiversity.

Deforestation & Wildfires

Higher temperatures dry out vegetation, creating the perfect tinder for massive, uncontrollable wildfires. These fires not only destroy crucial animal habitats and human homes, but they also release massive amounts of stored carbon back into the atmosphere, creating a dangerous positive feedback loop that further accelerates climate change.

Melting Polar Ice

The Arctic is warming at more than twice the global average rate. The dramatic decline in sea ice volume and extent is removing vital habitat for iconic species like polar bears, walruses, and seals. The thawing permafrost also risks releasing immense quantities of ancient methane—a potent greenhouse gas—into the atmosphere.

Causes and Effects of Climate Change

Authored by Thanujan

In this article:

  • - Introduction
  • - Primary Causes
  • - Global Effects
  • - Conclusion

Introduction to the Climate Crisis

Climate change means long-term changes in temperature and weather. Some changes happen naturally, but since the 1800s, human activities have been the main cause. Burning fuels like coal, oil, and gas releases gases that trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, making the planet warmer.



Primary Causes

The greenhouse effect helps keep Earth warm, which is important for life. But human activities are making it stronger than normal by adding too many gases to the air.

  • Fossil Fuels: Burning coal, oil, and gas for electricity, transport, and heating releases a lot of harmful gases.
  • Deforestation: Cutting down trees reduces the Earth’s ability to absorb carbon dioxide and also releases stored carbon.
  • Agriculture: Animals like cows and sheep produce methane, a strong gas that increases global warming.


Global Effects

Climate change is affecting the whole world, not just making it warmer. It is changing natural systems and human life.

We are already witnessing severe impacts, including:

  • More droughts, heatwaves, and wildfires are happening.
  • Sea levels are rising because ice is melting, which can flood coastal areas.
  • Oceans are becoming more acidic, harming sea life like coral reefs.
  • Changes in farming can affect food supply around the world.


Conclusion

Understanding the causes and effects of climate change is important. Even though the problem is serious, people can work together to reduce its impact and protect the future of our planet.

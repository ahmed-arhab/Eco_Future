Global Climate Policies and Future Actions

The Imperative for Global Cooperation

Because greenhouse gas emissions mix globally in the atmosphere, climate change is the ultimate tragedy of the commons—it cannot be solved by any single nation. It requires robust international frameworks, binding treaties, and unprecedented global cooperation to shift the economic paradigm towards sustainability.

The Paris Agreement (2015)

The Paris Agreement marked a historic turning point. For the first time, almost every country on earth signed a binding agreement to undertake ambitious efforts to combat climate change and adapt to its effects. Its central aim is to limit global temperature rise this century well below 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels, while pursuing efforts to limit the increase even further to 1.5 degrees.

Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs)

Under the Paris Agreement, countries submit comprehensive national climate action plans, known as NDCs. These plans outline specific actions countries will take to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions to reach the goals of the Paris Agreement. These pledges are reviewed and updated every five years, intended to scale up ambition over time.

Future Strategies & Economic Shifts

Moving forward, policy must transition from pledges to concrete implementation. Future strategies include carbon pricing (taxes and cap-and-trade systems), ending fossil fuel subsidies, and massive public investments in green infrastructure and clean energy research. Corporate accountability and sustainable financing are also becoming critical pillars of future climate action.
